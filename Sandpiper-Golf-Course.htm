Renovation at Sandpiper Golf Club

Plans for Tom Doak to re-design Sandpiper Golf Club in Santa Barbara(Goleta), California have been given hope.

The property faces the Channel Islands and aging offshore oil platforms scheduled to be decommissioned. Sandpiper was designed by William F. Bell and opened in 1972. The landscape has the advantage of natural terrain atop seaside cliffs. A new routing from Tom Doak to integrate the shoreline into its design is promising. The Bell layout is routed laterally to the clifftop for the majority of the journey with the exception of the downhill par 3 11th hole which plays downhill to a beachside green.

The new ideas do not have a construction start date yet. However, Doak posted to Instagram a photo in early 2021 of the 18th hole with the caption that the pond in front of the green would remain due to an threatened wildlife species.

Doak is a golf course architect who has designed many of the most famous courses in the world, including seaside links St. Patrick's in Ireland, Pacific Dunes in Oregon, and Tara Iti in New Zealand. The renovation could potentially include a number of changes, including changing the course's original routing, removing trees, improving drainage, renovating bunkers and greens.

Approvals for permitting may require California Coastal Commission involvement.
