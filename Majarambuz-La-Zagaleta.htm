Majarambuz Golf Club - La Zagaleta

CEO Jacobo Cestino of the La Zagaleta Group is developing Majarambuz Golf Club, a new Tom Doak designed golf course in Andalucia, Spain. It will be the first golf course designed by golf architect Tom Doak in Spain. The project has already obtained all the necessary permits to start construction. Preliminary designs have been finalized according to a statement by Valderrama 07, a subsidiary of La Zagaleta Group.

Locally, the mayor of Castellar, Adrián Vaca, has welcomed the announcement by praising the prestige of landing Tom Doak as its visionary designer. The project is still waiting for a minor modification entitlement approval from the Andalusian Advisory Council's General Urban Planning Plan (PGOU). The mayor has also requested infrastructure improvements to the local A-2100 highway as an important matter to connect Castellar with Sotogrande.

Property origins: Valderrama founder Jaime Ortiz-Patiño acquired properties La Cierva and La Guillena in 1998. In 2008, Mayor Vaca from Castellar approved the plans for the construction of a second Valderrama golf course. The 530+ acre Majarambuz Golf Club property is in the municipality of San Roque bordering Sotogrande adjacent to protected wilderness areas.

Majarambuz Golf Club is intended to be one of the most exclusive golf clubs in continental Europe. The club foresees a charter for a limited, invitation-only membership of about 300 with requirements of a low handicap, skilled player only to enjoy the course. Adjacent, the club intends to offer hotel and brand residence amenities for public visitors with access to the clubhouse restricted to members and their guests.
