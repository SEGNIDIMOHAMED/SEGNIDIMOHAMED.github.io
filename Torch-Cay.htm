Torch Cay Island - Penske - Coore Crenshaw Golf

Torch Cay MOU Signing Ceremony, Credit: ZNSNetwork

Torch Cay is an island located east of Little Exuma at the southernmost point of the Exuma island chain in the Bahamas. The island is the largest private island in the Bahamas with an area of 707 acres. Torch Cay Airport infrastructure is built to land with an ultra-long-range or heavy private jet.

In January 2023 the Government of the Bahamas signed a Heads of Agreement with Philip Penske of PMR Bahamas to develop an upscale resort and residential community on Torch Cay. The project will cost $170 million is expected to create hundreds of jobs during construction and operation.

The resort will build a golf course designed by Bill Coore & Ben Crenshaw. Build out of the resort is expected to take 8-10 years. Other amenities include a marina and boutique hotel. There will be a residential community with a mix of single family homes.

Climate on Torch Cay is tropical with average temperatures from 75°F to 85°F (24°C to 29°C) throughout the year. The wet season runs from May to October with an average of 60 inches (152 cm) of precipitation annually. The climate on Torch Cay is kept cool by trade winds from the northeast. Seawater temperature around the island is warm, averaging 80°F (27°C) year-round.

Soil type on Torch Cay is a mixture of sand, coral, and limestone. Sand comes from the erosion of the surrounding reefs. Coral and limestone are remnants of ancient coral reefs. The soil is relatively shallow, with an average depth of 6 feet (1.8 m). Sandy soil on Torch Cay may be prone to erosion from winds and tides.
