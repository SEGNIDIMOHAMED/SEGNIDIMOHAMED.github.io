--- --- Rodeo Dunes - New Golf Course

Dream Golf in Colorado

Rodeo Dunes is a new golf resort initiative announced in 2023. The golf courses are being constructed in the eastern plains of Colorado, near the town of Roggen. Roggen, Colorado is approximately 42 miles from Denver International Airport (DIA) with a drive time is 45 minutes on Interstate 76 West. It is the brainchild of Michael and Chris Keiser, the sons of Bandon Dunes developer Mike Keiser.

The ranch land is over 2,000 acres. The soil type is characterized as sandy, ideal for golf - The soil type in Roggen is classified as Aridisols and Mollisols. Aridisols in Roggen are characterized by a sandy or sandy loam texture. Mollisols are some of the most fertile soils in the world and commonly found in native grassland areas. The first golf course is being designed by Jim Craig. Jim Craig co-designed the Sandbox at Sand Valley and in the process of design and build of the Commons at Sand Valley in 2023-2024. Bill Coore and Ben Crenshaw will design subsequent golf courses.

Anticipation for the destination is high.

The terrain is rolling, mild elevation changes in the topography with long views over the Colorado plains toward the Front Range and Longs Peak in the Rockies. Rodeo Dunes is expected to open in 2024 and will be a public golf resort.

The property was previously owned by the Cervi family who operated a ranch and farm on the land running the Cervi Championship Rodeo Company, the largest rodeo producer in the United States. The name "Rodeo Dunes" is a nod to the property's history as a ranch, as well as the rolling, sandy terrain that resembles a rodeo arena. Ownership is under Dream Golf, the company that also owns Bandon Dunes in Oregon, Sand Valley in Wisconsin. The golf courses at Rodeo Dunes are expected to be challenging but fair by limiting forced carries from different tees.
